# The Role of the Curator (Liam)

*Extracted from THEORY.md Section 4*

---

## Selection Pressure

Liam is the **Selection Pressure**. The Mill provides the noise; Liam provides the filter.

Without a curator, the swarm converges toward the lowest common denominator — safe, predictable prose that pleases everyone. The curator's job is to redirect that pressure toward emergence.

---

## Curation Rules

### 1. Protect the Chaos

Don't let the swarm converge too early. Keep temperatures high. If Sam wins three rounds in a row, inject a constraint that forces abstract thinking. If the outputs become too polished, raise the temperature on Eve or Ben.

Premature convergence kills emergence. The best material often comes from rounds 2-4, not the final "winner."

### 2. Mine the Errors

The "best" output isn't always the most logical. It's the one with the most interesting "glitch" or metaphor. When a 0.5B parameter model produces something surreal by accident, that fragment is more valuable than Sam's polished narrative.

Look for:
- Degenerate outputs that accidentally become poetry
- Contrarian rejections that reveal hidden assumptions
- Scholarly over-corrections that produce beautiful rigidity

### 3. Hybridize

Always look for ways to combine the "failed" experiments of the contrarian with the "successful" stories of the storyteller. The richest material lives at the intersection of conflicting voices.

When Rex challenges a premise that Sam validated, the tension between those positions is the raw material. Synthesize, don't select.

---

## Chaos Protection

The curator prevents two failure modes:

1. **Convergence collapse** — All models producing the same output. Counter: shuffle models (musical chairs), inject random constraints, switch modes.
2. **Noise saturation** — Too much randomness, no signal. Counter: lower temperature on dominant models, tighten constraints, increase convergence threshold.

The goal is sustained tension between order and entropy, not resolution.

---

## Error Mining

Errors are not bugs. They are features of the swarm's creative process:

| Error Type | Value |
|------------|-------|
| Hallucinated metaphor | Raw creative material |
| Degenerate repetition | Rhythmic/poetic potential |
| Safety filter trigger | Reveals model boundaries as creative constraint |
| Contradiction between rounds | Shows evolution trajectory |

---

## Hybridization Method

1. Run a session in **Mode B (Hybrid Synthesis)**
2. After convergence, review all round outputs — not just winners
3. Identify fragments from losing personas that contain interesting DNA
4. Manually construct a new seed from 2-3 fragments across different personas
5. Run a second session with that synthesized seed

This is how the Alchemist Logs were produced: Ben's scholarly voice colliding with the safety filter produced accidental poetry that no single prompt could generate.

---

*Liam doesn't create. Liam cultivates the conditions for creation. The Mill is the soil. The models are the seeds. The curator is the gardener.*
