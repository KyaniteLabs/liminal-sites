# The Warpered Echo

*A synthesis of Mill evolutions*

The world was a warped mirror—
its edges blurring into grays and whites,
carefully crafted pieces spun
like a kaleidoscope of selves.

I wore each one with practiced ease,
threadbare fabric,
aonymous cloak,
the face stretched thin by laughter
forced into shape.

Then: the last note.
It hung suspended in the air,
a shimmering melody stretched
across an echo-laden stage.

What remains when the performance ends?
Not the mask.
Not the applause.

Something older.
A warm hum rooted in time,
the scent of paper aged with untold stories,
grandmother's attic dust motes
dancing in sunlight.

The mirror warps.
The echo fades.

The world was harsh.
I had mastered the art
of becoming invisible.

But in the silence,
a phantom touch lingers—
laughter mingling with tears,
glistening trails on cheeks
red from holding back
what was always already
there.
