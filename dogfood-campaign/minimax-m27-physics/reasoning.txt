**Self-Correction during thought process:**
Standard elastic collision formulas can be tricky with varying masses.
Formula for 2D collision (equal mass):
`v1' = v1 - (v1-v2) . (x1-x2) / |x1-x2|^2 * (x1-x2)`
Let's use the simpler "swap velocities along normal" approximation or the standard formula.
Let's use the standard vector formula:
`v1_new = v1 - (2*m2/(m1+m2)) * dot(v1-v2, x1-x2) / |x1-x2|^2 * (x1-x2)`
Since they are balls, mass can be `r^2` (area).
To keep code clean, I will treat mass as `1` (or equal) to avoid complex math, or just use the elastic collision formula with mass proportional to radius for slightly better feel.
